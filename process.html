Manufacturing Journey

Step-by-Step Excellence

Our systematic approach guarantees precise, defect-free iron casting production at every stage.

Step 01

Pattern & Mould Design

Our engineering team utilizes advanced CAD/CAM software to design accurate patterns based on customer drawings, ensuring perfect dimensional accuracy and optimized casting yields from the start.

CAD/CAM 3D Modeling Pattern Shop

Design Specifications

  • CAD Platform SolidWorks / AutoCAD
  • Pattern Material Alu / Wood / Resin
  • Draft Angle 1.5 Degrees Max
  • Validation Solidification Analysis
Step 02

Sand Moulding & Preparation

Using our semi-automatic Green Sand moulding line, we prepare high-integrity sand moulds. We support mould box sizes up to 600×700×200mm, allowing us to produce components ranging from 0.7 Kg to 100 Kg with high repeatability.

Green Sand Line Mould Box Core Shop

Moulding Capabilities

  • Process Line Semi-Auto Green Sand
  • Max Box Size 600 × 700 × 200 mm
  • Moulding Speed 25 - 30 Moulds/hr
  • Weight Range 0.7 Kg to 100 Kg
Step 03

Melting & Alloying

Scrap iron and alloys are melted in induction furnaces powered by a 350 kW control panel. We operate dual crucibles of 500 Kg and 300 Kg capacity, with direct spectrometer chemical composition checks before tapping.

350 kW Panel Dual Crucibles Spectrography

Melting Specifications

  • Furnace Type Medium Freq Induction
  • Crucible Capacities 500 Kg & 300 Kg
  • Chemical Control Optical Spectrometer
  • Average Temp 1450°C Tapping
Step 04

Pouring & Solidification

Liquid metal is transferred via overhead crane and poured using 500 Kg to 1000 Kg ladles. We utilize a clean-pouring T-spout ladle design to block slag and ensure clean metal flows into the mould cavities.

T-Spout Ladle Overhead Crane Controlled Solidification

Pouring Capabilities

  • Pouring Method Crane & Geared Ladle
  • Ladle Capacities 500 Kg – 1000 Kg
  • Ladle Design T-Spout (Slag-Free)
  • Inoculation Late In-Mould Inoculation
Step 05

Shakeout & Fettling

After cooling, castings are demoulded via a shakeout deck. The parts are fettled using swing and flexible shaft grinders to remove runners and risers, followed by shot-blasting to achieve a clean surface finish.

Shot Blasting Grinding Shop Vibratory Deck

Fettling Equipment

  • Shot Blasting Type Tumble Blast Machine
  • Swing Grinders 2 × Heavy Duty Units
  • Shaft Grinders 25 × Flexible Shafts
  • Hand Grinders 10 × Pneumatic & Electric
Step 06

Precision Machining

We provide in-house precision machining. Our machine shop features CNC turning centers and 3-axis VMC (Vertical Machining Centers) to execute complex contours, mill pockets, drill holes, and tap threads to customer drawing limits.

3-Axis VMC CNC Turning Finished Parts

Machining Capabilities

  • VMC Type 3-Axis High-Speed VMC
  • CNC Turning Slant Bed CNC Lathes
  • Operations Turning, Boring, Threading
  • Tolerances Up to Custom Fine Limits
Step 07

Quality Testing & Dispatch

Prior to shipment, castings undergo complete material and sand laboratory tests, spectrometer checks, mechanical hardness/tensile strength trials, and dimensional validation using CMM and profiles.

Spectrometer CMM Check Sand Testing

Quality Controls

  • Chemical Test Direct Reading Spectro
  • Mechanical Test Hardness & Tensile Bars
  • Mould Properties Sand Permeability & Strength
  • Dimensions Coordinate Measuring Machine
